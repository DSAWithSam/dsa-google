# Hard Track - Google Playlist

This folder contains the **5 senior-level Hard problems** that frequently appear in Google onsite interviews (especially for L4/L5 roles).

These problems are significantly more challenging and test your ability to combine multiple patterns, optimize under constraints, and design efficient data structures.

### Problems

| # | Problem | Key Patterns | Link |
|---|---------|--------------|------|
| 1 | LRU Cache | Hash Map + Doubly Linked List | [0146-lru-cache.md](./0146-lru-cache.md) |
| 2 | Trapping Rain Water | Two Pointers / Stack / Greedy | [0042-trapping-rain-water.md](./0042-trapping-rain-water.md) |
| 3 | Median of Two Sorted Arrays | Binary Search / Divide & Conquer | [0004-median-of-two-sorted-arrays.md](./0004-median-of-two-sorted-arrays.md) |
| 4 | Word Search II | Trie + DFS Backtracking | [0212-word-search-ii.md](./0212-word-search-ii.md) |
| 5 | Find Median from Data Stream | Heaps (Priority Queues) | [0295-find-median-from-data-stream.md](./0295-find-median-from-data-stream.md) |

### Recommended Order

Follow this order when studying:

1. **LRU Cache** Most frequently asked Hard at Google (17× reported). Master design + linked list + hash map.
2. **Trapping Rain Water** Excellent for understanding multiple approaches (stack, two pointers, greedy).
3. **Median of Two Sorted Arrays** Advanced binary search and divide & conquer.
4. **Word Search II** Combines Trie with backtracking (builds on Word Search).
5. **Find Median from Data Stream** Heavy use of heaps for real-time median finding.

### Notes

- These problems are significantly harder than Medium. Expect to spend more time understanding the intuition.
- Focus on **why** a particular data structure or technique is optimal.
- Many of these problems combine multiple patterns you learned in Easy and Medium.

Mastering this Hard track will give you a strong edge in Google onsite interviews.
